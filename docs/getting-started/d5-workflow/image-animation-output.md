### &#x20;**Image**

After configuring the FOV, resolution, and channel maps, you can proceed to render images.

Moreover, you have the option to enhance rendered images using AI. The AI Enhancer optimizes materials, characters, fog, and many other subtle details to elevate your images.

#### AI Style Transfer

We have integrated an AI post-production tool into D5 that allows you to transform a rendered image into various styles with just one click.&#x20;

Aftering rendering a test image, you can proceed to conduct AI style transfer.

### Animation

The timeline in D5 consists of two essential segments: one for controlling camera movement and the other for sequencing objects, each containing relevant templates.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXeWm59cWWalXsNYg2Cig-OvTrA27NBCcqFed6Wb_RYgGabXrVhaKPB_2GLq9zgJ-N_WRN34h_IMl9OaCGiMH3s2BHEec9QyCwXbOPJXCX7465-x5hksTAGDGhhrKAg78c9ouBPx?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

#### Camera Movement

When you need to create a shot, simply choose your desired template and D5 will generate a shot based on the current view.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXd43PM_4rxNpYh0dVHUDv4qA8e-4uuNCa-yR76Favfz7JtIHQptwoCa4Cuz93UBMUwyy0BmYjQFS9NnNhG0fdNmS-2ORb92pXg3yi55nmf6THKHkYaDFx1vX_M40c_hVDaXJpY?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can freely edit the path to achieve advanced camera movement.

For a 360° panning shot, for instance, you can adjust the radius to expand the path.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXe7vylSUc1jV1bSQ1iRtY7Ov5Fak1gZdh8XPRrewD-7URxuxSLn0OtbyEvTTRhWX2OOGATBD-xEA5CBqLTtLpHDXWBW7rFDz1W2WaTSQvii5HAkb58q2gTFP7226ojpAt4doHU?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can freely move the path to your desired position and use the PiP window to review the camera's perspective.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXc9_BCpWP5WCfPag-5TwrWVyopBEA8BYX7BJIqEUIPHVpGcAxNN3hZ0DEOjAV-33-5Lx4U6YFb7jnMWzQ4o1OSijPKg9Z0deu3M8Ztx_36xii3LRECbrpWu5HVhV6ezWBXEbg7-?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Next, modify the movement degree which determines the travel distance for your camera. Now, a rotating camera is set up.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfsNT_E5XGNQXdARdZ5nbbcbJUcZq9VP4xYpLB0258I_wa-XKN1uJKxbtU05prbevfM50_-9zLKFfY1bS547rDc3Mlt3EjkVVPF_H9AL72dGQF3a46eP7DVyqGu48hJ6vYvziFj?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you prefer to start from scratch, simply add a view as the initial frame, adjust the camera, and set the final frame. A shot will be generated accordingly.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXc9Z0z8Ywt0p_yGYMQvYOMOe7b2aXiodq9b1KkpVWJ2Z5DmL3j63t6KRgO5kEeihq8sdsgcPPbM1r-hAZTInY0d43uk5FDangYLvGlAxSJ8VQNwVniXWqVzQvsvSalctc_ES6yU?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

#### Phasing Animation Effects

<mark style="background-color:green;">Watch the tutorial for phasing animation:</mark>&#x20;

[<mark style="background-color:green;">Phasing Animation Tutorial | D5 Render</mark>](https://youtu.be/mc4Bhzc9aDg?si=Xgm4tTsb8QihJ3hU)

D5 Render supports creating phasing animations using templates.

> *\*Note that you can use D5 LiveSync plugins for SketchUp, Revit, Rhino, and 3ds Max to export models while retaining their group/layer/family structure after importing them to D5. Support for more workflows will be updated in the future.*

After selecting your desired effect, you can click to add the objects for animation. D5 Render enables you to add objects individually or in groups. Under the “add by group” mode, you can click a group to add all the models it includes.

<figure><img src="https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXdIOSMnbNXnc_gxq_EzakG_fup7635vaK_AeGRm0TkXKpn4Fj2EQpGOteDxy-TiFIBiRwoFmmY7J3q7EAscy1bCbnbVqlh8GhgfUr4TmDNhUqjKvQiAHsvYtqBNge77XqzmzQBp?key=Fj3gEvh4f7dQWs61CsH4SzuA" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can customize whether they should appear based on the order of the object list or their location in the world coordinates.
